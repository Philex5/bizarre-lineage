# Raid in Bizarre Lineage

In Bizarre Lineage, raids are an 8-player cooperative mode. Raids are built for teams to clear harder content together, then spend raid-specific tokens in the Raid Shop on exclusive rewards.

This page is based on the current public raid and region notes, checked on February 27, 2026.

## Quick answer

Raids matter because they are one of the clearest mid-game and late-game reward routes for:

- raid shop tokens
- Lucky Arrows
- Legendary Chests
- Stand Arrows
- traits
- raid-exclusive accessories and weapons

If you are still fixing your first stand, first stat route, or early progression, raids are usually not the first thing to force. They make more sense once you can survive, contribute damage, and farm with a group.

## How raids work

The wording is straightforward: raids are a cooperative mode for 8 players, and completion gives you rewards plus raid shop tokens. Those tokens are not shared across every raid. Each raid has its own token economy and its own shop list.

That means the practical question is not just "Should I raid?" but also "Which raid shop has the item I actually want?"

## What to farm for

If your goal is efficient utility value first, the raid shops make a few things obvious:

- Stand Arrows are the cheapest entries across every listed raid shop.
- Legendary Chests sit in the mid-cost range, but Avdol's version is much more expensive than the others.
- Lucky Arrows are always expensive single-stock purchases.
- Unique accessories, traits, and weapons are the main reason to target one raid over another.

## Common raid mistake

The common mistake is treating raids like early-game carry content. They are reward-focused group content. In practice, that means you usually get more value by fixing your stand, stats, and progression basics first, then farming the raid whose shop actually matches your target reward.
